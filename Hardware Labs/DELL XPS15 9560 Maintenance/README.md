# Dell XPS 15 9560 Laptop Repair and Maintenance

## Introduction

This lab will highlight how to troubleshoot and replace a faulty Wi-Fi Card and Battery for Dell XPS 15 9560 Laptop.

## Wi-Fi Card Troubleshooting

The Killer1535 Wi-Fi Adapter in the Dell XPS 15 9560 was malfunctioning and needed to be replaced. 

The Wi-Fi on the computer started to drop signals every few seconds. Before concluding it was a hardware issue, I troubleshoot the Wi-Fi by:

1) Turning the Wi-Fi on and off

2) Ran the the Wi-Fi Troubleshooter and see if it find or resolve the problem.

3) Confirm that the Router was connected and other devices were able to connect.

4) Forget and Reconnect the Wi-Fi's SSID

5) Confirm the Wireless Adapter driver was up to date

6) Used an USB external Wi-Fi adapter to see if the laptop was able to recieve and hold the signal.

After all other method has failed and the USB External Wi-Fi adapter was able to recieve and hold a signal. I concluded that the Wi-Fi Card hardware was faulty and needed replacement.

## Wi-Fi Adapter Replacement

### Tools and Equipments

- M2 Torx Screwdriver
- Philips Screwdriver 
- NCGIGA AX210NGW Wi-Fi 6E Card
- Anti-Static Wristband and Mat

To replace the Killer1535 Wi-Fi Adapter, flip the laptop around and unscrew the cover using the appropriate size Torx screwdriver. For the Dell XPS 15 9560, there are also two Philips screws underneath the middle cover. Ensure all screws are kept in a container to prevent losing them.

>**Note**: Always use ESD protections when working on hardwares (ie. ESD Wristband and Mat), and never work on a laptop plugged in to the power.

After taking off the cover, identify where the Wi-Fi card is located. For the Dell XPS 15 9560, the Wi-Fi card is located at the top left corner. There would be two wires connected to it and a plate with a screw to hold it down.

Unscrew the plate and using tweezers remove the two antenna wires from the old Wi-Fi card. For the Dell XPS 15 9560, the white wire is the main antenna, and the black wire is the aux. Then the Wi-Fi card should be able to be pulled of the M.2 PCIe slot. 

Once the new card is inserted, reconnect the antenna wires, making sure the main and aux wires corrosponds the same with the antenna slots on the new card. Then screw the card plate and put the cover back on (don't screw the cover back on until you ensure the Wi-Fi card is working properly).

Using the USB external Wi-Fi adapter or download separately in a flashdrive, download the install the new Wi-Fi card adapter driver for both Wi-Fi and Bluetooth. 

When installtion is successful, the laptop should be connecting properly to the Wi-Fi and working correctly again.

## Battery Replacement

The Dell XPS 15 9560 battery has been charging continuously even when reaching full capacity, accelerating the battery to degradation until it can no longer hold a charge.

The Dell XPS 15 9560 for this specific model uses a 97Kwh battery with a specific model number of: 6GTPY

### Tools and Equipments

-M2 Torx Screwdriver
-Philips Screwdriver
-6GTPY 97Kwh battery
-Anti-Static Wristband and Mat

To replace the battery in the Dell XPS 15 9560 laptop, flip it over and unscrew the the cover using the Torx screwdriver. As mention previously, there are two Philips screwdriver underneath the middle cover.

>**Note**: Always use ESD protections when working on hardwares (ie. ESD Wristband and Mat), and never work on a laptop plugged in to the power.

Once the cover is off, locate the battery at the bottom part of the laptop. Disconnect the power wire connector connecting it to the laptop, and then unscrew the screws holding the battery down.

Install the new battery. Screw the screws back down and reconnect the power wire connection. Put the cover back on and test the laptop and make sure that it is working properly before screwing the cover securely on.
